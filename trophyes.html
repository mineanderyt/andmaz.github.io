Participation in National/International Championships

UEFA Champions League
The UEFA Champions League, also known as the European Cup, is the most prestigious official international football tournament at club level in Europe. Organized by its continental establishment, UEFA, it has been held annually since its inception in the 1955-56 season, and its final is the most followed sporting event in the world, with an estimated audience of between 350 and 400 million viewers, ahead of the NFL and the NBA; Valencia has reached two finals, but was beaten by their opponents.
"La Copa"
The Spanish Championship of His Majesty the King's Cup, better known by its simple meanings of Copa del Rey or La Copa, is a national knockout football competition, organized annually by the Royal Spanish Football Federation and played by 116 clubs from Spain. ; The last one that Valencia won was in 2019 coinciding with the centenary of the club.
"LaLiga"
The Spanish Primera División or LaLiga known as LaLiga Santander for sponsorship reasons and whose official name is Campeonato Nacional de Liga de Primera División is the top division of the Spanish football league system and the main club-level competition in the country. It has been organized since 1984 by the National Professional Soccer League after succeeding the Royal Spanish Soccer Federation (RFEF). It has been held since the 1928-29 season and was only interrupted once: between 1936 and 1938, due to the Spanish Civil War.
Spanish Super Cup
The Spanish Super Cup is an official football competition organized by the Royal Spanish Football Federation, played since 1982 and since 2020, pits the top two finishers of the First Division League and the two finalists of the Copa del Rey from the previous season. The immediate official precedent was the so-called Eva Duarte Cup, organized by the Royal Spanish Football Federation between 1947 and 1953.
UEFA Europa League
The UEFA Europa League, originally known as the UEFA Cup, is a continental club competition organized by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA), considered the second most prestigious in Europe, after the Champions League and one of the three continental competitions organized by UEFA, along with the aforementioned and the Conference League.
European Super Cup
The UEFA Super Cup, known as the European Super Cup, is a continental club competition organized by the Union of European Football Associations (UEFA), which pits the champions of Europe's two highest continental competitions: the Champions League and the Europa League.
UEFA Cup Winners' Cup
The UEFA Cup Winners' Cup or European Cup Winners' Cup until 1994, was an official international football competition organized by UEFA that, for 39 seasons, was played by the winners of the national cup competitions of different European countries. Although the 1960-61 edition, won by Fiorentina of Italy, is considered the first of the tournament, it was not recognized by UEFA until October 1963 at the request of the Italian Federation, because its organization fell to the Mitropa Cup committee. The European confederation was directly in charge of organizing the tournament from 1961 to 1999, the year in which the Cup Winners' Cup was absorbed by the UEFA Cup.
