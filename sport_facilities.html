MESTALLA STADIUM

ABOUT MESTALLA

The prestigious British newspaper 'The Telegraph' has published a special content in its sports area about the best 20 stadiums in European football regarding the intention of FC Barcelona to remodel theirs for the 2020/21 season. In this ranking, the Camp de Mestalla occupies no less than second place. 'The Telegraph' defines the VCF fans as “the most demanding in Spain with the most atmosphere” and that it has “the stands with the greatest inclination compared to any major stadium in Europe that leave you breathless, even if you've seen them a thousand times".

The black and white coliseum has been above the Spanish stadiums Camp Nou, Santiago Bernabéu and Vicente Calderón, and mythical coliseums such as Parque de los Príncipes de Paris, Anfield, Allianz Arena, Old Trafford, Giuseppe Meazza and is only surpassed by the Signal Iduna Park in Dortmund.

SPORT CITY PATERNA

THE HISTORY OF THE SPORT CITY

Valencia acquired the land in Paterna during the presidency of Francisco Ros Casares in 1974. Without a doubt, this acquisition was the best legacy of this president, who tried to apply his business vision to the club and went to modernize it.

The Paterna lands were a great success. Until the birth of the Paterna fields, the Valencia players did their preparatory sessions in unfavorable conditions for a team of its category. The training sessions took place on a piece of land located behind the numbered stands, which did not measure the regulatory dimensions and, exceptionally, used the Mestalla field. Sometimes, the Valencian players trained at the Benimar facilities.

Despite the hardships, Ros Casares and his board faced numerous opposition to this project. The team trained by the "Saeta Rubia" was going through a bad sporting situation, so the fans were more concerned about that than about the board's commitment to the future.

The shareholders' meeting, after endless discussions, gave the green light to the acquisition of the land for a price of seventy million pesetas. Ros Casares could not even sign the deed of sale since, when the time came to formalize it, he had already left the presidency. The final price was 85 million pesetas, after the acquisition of related land.

Ros Casares and his team showed great vision in this operation, since the condition they had imposed was that the sports city grounds should be close to the future bypass, and for this reason they chose Paterna.

The current Ciudad Deportiva, after the remodeling, was born on February 19, 1992. Over time, the grounds were conditioned as a training field for the first team and as a soccer school.

The Sports City covers an area of 180,000m2. In addition to being a school for future soccer players and a residence, it is conceived as a modern Rehabilitation Center equipped with several rooms for the recovery of soccer players who are injured or who are in the process of rehabilitation.

There are some conditioned rooms, such as those for physiotherapists, the hydromassage room, sauna and swimming pool. The spacious gym, with a wide variety of the most modern equipment, is an ideal complement for the correct physical preparation of the soccer players.

The central building has, on the first floor, ten rooms to house the players, as well as a living room and a dining room. The audiovisual room allows the coach to work with video editing, to analyze his team's game and study the rival teams. The first team and subsidiary changing rooms are located on the ground floor.

Behind the central building there is a field with a grandstand that can hold about 2,250 spectators, the place where Valencia CF - Mestalla play their matches. For its part, the Valencia first team has several grass pitches for its usual training sessions, as well as areas exclusively reserved for the first team.

The Valencia Club de Fútbol sports facilities continue to grow by providing greater coverage to the hundreds of athletes who use them every day. The latest additions have taken the form of training ramps (empowerment) and a foot volleyball field.

The Sports City of Valencia is considered one of the best in Europe, at the same level as other legendary soccer schools.
